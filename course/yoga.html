Yoga Admission Process in India

Step-by-step guide to secure admission in top yoga colleges across India.

1

Research & Choose Course

Select your preferred yoga specialization (Hatha, Ashtanga, Therapy, etc.) based on interests and career goals.

2

Check Eligibility

Ensure you meet 10+2 requirements with minimum marks. Age between 17-25 years for undergraduate.

3

Apply to Colleges

Submit applications to chosen colleges with required documents. Many offer online application forms.

4

Entrance Exams (if required)

Some universities conduct entrance exams or interviews. Prepare with yoga basics and theory.

5

Document Verification

Submit 10th/12th certificates, ID proof, medical certificate, and other required documents.

6

Fee Payment & Admission

Pay admission fees and complete enrollment. Check for scholarships and fee waiver options.

7

Orientation & Classes

Attend orientation program and start your yoga education with practical sessions.

8

Career Guidance

Get ongoing support for internships, teaching opportunities, and higher studies in yoga.

Important Entrance Exams

University Entrance Exams

Specific exams conducted by universities like S-VYASA, Patanjali University, etc.

Merit-Based Admission

Many colleges offer admission based on 10+2 marks without entrance exams.

Interview/Practical Test

Some institutions require yoga demonstration or personal interview.

AYUSH Entrance

For yoga therapy programs, AYUSH entrance exams may be applicable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about yoga admissions, courses, and career prospects.

What is the eligibility for B.Yoga in India?

You need to have completed 10+2 with any stream and minimum 50% marks. Age should be between 17-25 years. No specific subject requirements.

Which entrance exam is required for yoga courses?

Most yoga courses have merit-based admission. Some universities conduct their own entrance exams or interviews.

Graduates can work as Yoga Instructors, Therapists, Teachers in schools/colleges, Wellness centers, and start their own yoga studios.

Yes, various scholarships are available including merit-based, state government scholarships, and those for SC/ST/OBC students.
