LLB Eligibility & Qualifications in India

LLB (Bachelor of Laws) is a 3-year undergraduate law degree for graduates, while BA LLB/B.Com LLB/BBA LLB are 5-year integrated programs for 12th pass students. Here's the detailed eligibility and qualifications:

For 3-Year LLB

  • Qualification: Bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university
  • Minimum Marks: 45-50% aggregate (relaxation for reserved categories)
  • Age Limit: No upper age limit (varies by college)
  • Entrance Exams: CLAT, AILET, LSAT, state CETs

For 5-Year Integrated LLB

  • Qualification: 10+2 (12th) from recognized board
  • Minimum Marks: 45-50% in 12th (relaxation for reserved categories)
  • Age Limit: 20-22 years (varies by college)
  • Entrance Exams: CLAT, AILET, LSAT, state CETs

Additional Qualifications & Requirements

  • Reservation: SC/ST/OBC/PWD categories get relaxation in marks and age
  • Documents: 10th/12th mark sheets, graduation certificate (for 3-year), ID proof, caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Skills: Good communication, analytical thinking, research skills
  • Career Prospects: Lawyer, Judge, Legal Advisor, Corporate Counsel, etc.
