Medical Admission Process in India

Step-by-step guide to secure admission in top medical colleges for MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS, and other medical programs.

1

Choose Medical Course

Select your preferred course such as MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS, BPT, or Nursing based on interest.

2

Check Eligibility

Ensure eligibility with 10+2 (PCB), minimum qualifying marks, and age criteria as per medical councils.

3

Appear for NEET

Appear for NEET-UG (for MBBS/BDS/AYUSH) or NEET-PG (for MD/MS) conducted at the national level.

4

Register for Counseling

Register for AIQ, state-level, or private medical college counseling through official portals.

5

Choice Filling & Seat Allotment

Fill college preferences and receive seat allotment based on NEET rank, category, and availability.

6

Document Verification

Verify documents including mark sheets, NEET scorecard, ID proof, domicile, and medical fitness.

7

Fee Payment

Pay tuition and security fees to confirm your medical seat within the given timeline.

8

Join Medical College

Report to the allotted college, complete admission formalities, and begin your medical education journey.

Medical Admission FAQ

What is the eligibility for MBBS in India?

10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB), minimum 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/OBC), NEET UG qualification, and age between 17-25 years.

Which entrance exam is required for medical courses?

NEET UG for MBBS/BDS, AIIMS/INI CET for some institutes, state CETs for state quota seats. Some private colleges may have their own entrance tests.

Do you help with NEET counseling?

Yes, we provide free counseling for NEET preparation, college selection, admission process, and scholarship guidance.

How long does the MBBS course take?

MBBS is 5.5 years including 1 year internship. BDS is 5 years, BAMS/BHMS/BUMS/BNYS are 5.5 years, Nursing is 4 years, BPT/BOT are 4.5 years.
