Eligibility & Qualifications for Medical Courses

General requirements for admission to medical degree colleges in India. Specific criteria may vary by college and state.

Academic Qualifications

  • 10+2 or equivalent from recognized board
  • Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB) as compulsory subjects
  • Minimum aggregate marks: 50% (45% for SC/ST/OBC)
  • English as a subject in 10+2
  • Age limit: 17-25 years (varies by course)

Entrance Exams

  • NEET UG for MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS, BUMS, BNYS
  • AIIMS/INI CET for AIIMS and other institutes
  • State CETs for state quota seats
  • JIPMER for Puducherry institute
  • Some private colleges may have their own exams

Additional Requirements

  • Medical fitness certificate
  • Character certificate from school/college
  • Migration certificate (if applicable)
  • Category certificate for reservations (SC/ST/OBC)
  • Income certificate for fee concessions

Medical Admission FAQ

What is the eligibility for MBBS in India?

10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB), minimum 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/OBC), NEET UG qualification, and age between 17-25 years.

Which entrance exam is required for medical courses?

NEET UG for MBBS/BDS, AIIMS/INI CET for some institutes, state CETs for state quota seats. Some private colleges may have their own entrance tests.

How long does the MBBS course take?

MBBS is 5.5 years including 1 year internship. BDS is 5 years, BAMS/BHMS/BUMS/BNYS are 5.5 years, Nursing is 4 years, BPT/BOT are 4.5 years.
