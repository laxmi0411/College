Eligibility & Qualifications for Pharmacy Courses

General requirements for admission to pharmacy degree colleges in India. Specific criteria may vary by college and state.

Academic Qualifications

  • 10+2 or equivalent from recognized board
  • Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology as compulsory subjects
  • Minimum aggregate marks: 50% (45% for SC/ST/OBC)
  • English as a subject in 10+2
  • Age limit: 17-23 years (varies by course)

Entrance Exams

  • GPAT for M.Pharm admissions
  • State CETs for B.Pharm and D.Pharm
  • NEET for Pharm.D in some states
  • Some private colleges may have their own exams

Additional Requirements

  • Medical fitness certificate
  • Character certificate from school/college
  • Migration certificate (if applicable)
  • Category certificate for reservations (SC/ST/OBC)
  • Income certificate for fee concessions

GPAT for M.Pharm, state CETs for B.Pharm/D.Pharm, NEET for Pharm.D in some states. Some private colleges have their own entrance tests.
