Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about architecture admissions, courses, and career prospects.

What is the eligibility for B.Arch in India?

You need to have completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics with minimum 50% marks. Age should be between 17-23 years. English must be a subject in 10+2.

Which entrance exam is required for architecture courses?

NATA is the main entrance exam for B.Arch programs. JEE Paper 2 for IITs and NITs. State CETs like KCET, TS EAMCET, etc., are required for state colleges. Some universities conduct their own entrance exams.

Do you help with architecture admission counseling?

Yes, we provide free counseling for architecture courses. Our experts help with college selection, entrance exam preparation, document verification, and admission process guidance.

What are the job opportunities after B.Arch?

Graduates can work as Architects, Urban Planners, Interior Designers, Project Managers, and in construction firms. Higher studies options include M.Arch and related fields.

Are there scholarships available for architecture students?

Yes, various scholarships are available including COA scholarships, state government scholarships, merit-based scholarships, and those for SC/ST/OBC students. Private colleges also offer merit scholarships.

Can I do B.Arch without Mathematics?

Mathematics is compulsory for B.Arch. You need PCM in 10+2. Check specific college requirements as they may vary.

What are the top private architecture colleges in India?

Some reputed private colleges include CEPT University, Lovely Professional University, Chandigarh University, Amity University, and Jain University. They offer good infrastructure and placement support.
