Eligibility & Qualifications for Agriculture Courses

General requirements for admission to agriculture degree colleges in India. Specific criteria may vary by college and state.

B.Sc Agriculture

  • 10+2 with PCB/PCM, min 50% marks
  • ICAR AIEEA or state entrance exams
  • Age: 17-23 years
  • English as a subject in 10+2

B.Tech Agricultural Engineering

  • 10+2 with PCM, min 50% marks
  • JEE Main or state CETs
  • Age: 17-23 years
  • Mathematics compulsory

B.Sc Horticulture

  • 10+2 with PCB, min 50% marks
  • ICAR AIEEA or direct admission
  • Age: 17-23 years
  • Biology preferred

B.Sc Forestry

  • 10+2 with PCB, min 50% marks
  • ICAR AIEEA or state exams
  • Age: 17-23 years
  • Physical fitness required

B.Sc Dairy Science

  • 10+2 with PCB/PCM, min 50% marks
  • ICAR AIEEA or merit-based
  • Age: 17-23 years
  • Science stream mandatory

B.Sc Fisheries

  • 10+2 with PCB, min 50% marks
  • ICAR AIEEA or state entrance
  • Age: 17-23 years
  • Biology essential

Additional Requirements

  • Medical fitness certificate
  • Character certificate from school/college
  • Migration certificate (if applicable)
  • Category certificate for reservations (SC/ST/OBC)
  • Income certificate for fee concessions

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about agriculture admissions, courses, and career prospects.

What is the eligibility for B.Sc Agriculture in India?

You need to have completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics with minimum 50% marks. Age should be between 17-23 years. English must be a subject in 10+2.

Which entrance exam is required for agriculture courses?

ICAR AIEEA is the main entrance exam for central universities and ICAR accredited colleges. State CETs like KCET, TS EAMCET, etc., are required for state colleges. Some universities conduct their own entrance exams.

Do you help with agriculture admission counseling?

Yes, we provide free counseling for agriculture courses. Our experts help with college selection, entrance exam preparation, document verification, and admission process guidance.

What are the job opportunities after B.Sc Agriculture?

Graduates can work as Agricultural Officers, Farm Managers, Research Scientists, Extension Officers, Quality Control Inspectors, and in agribusiness companies. Higher studies options include M.Sc Agriculture and MBA Agribusiness.

Are there scholarships available for agriculture students?

Yes, various scholarships are available including ICAR scholarships, state government scholarships, merit-based scholarships, and those for SC/ST/OBC students. Private colleges also offer merit scholarships.

Can I do B.Sc Agriculture without Biology?

Some colleges accept Mathematics instead of Biology, but Biology is preferred for most agriculture courses. Check specific college requirements as they may vary.
